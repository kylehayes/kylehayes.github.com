Blame It on the alcohol...really?

Feb 24th, 2011 | Comments

Glee’s “Blame it on the Alcohol” episode this week was a considerable disappointment. I speak, of course, to the influential alcohol consumption by the students of the high school. To be clear I don’t have a problem with enjoying alcoholic drinks when one is 1) at a legal drinking age and 2) responsible about it. This episode showcased that it’s ok to drink as long as you are safe regardless of the legality and it also said there’s a chance you could get a way with it and if you do, that makes it ok.

I will be the first to say that I by no means expect Glee to preach a morale message on anything morale. However, alcohol is not something to joke about or even shed light on in a silly manner. The point is that underaged teens are not allowed to drink for reasonâ€”they are not responsible enough to consume a body altering agent at whim. The consequences for the students on Glee were only but a bit of humiliation on stage with a bunch of vomiting in front of their peers…big deal.

It’s sad to me that shows that are as influential as Glee is harness their power in ways that are going to turn heads to the creators of the show for their own fame, glory, and preaching their personal agenda to the masses. Glee could do so much for its audience that would be good for everybody. It doesn’t need to have sex and alcohol to make a statement. I’ve been disappointed with a couple of other previous episodes of Glee but won’t comment on those, but this one really irked me due to the dangerous nature of alcohol in irresponsible hands.
